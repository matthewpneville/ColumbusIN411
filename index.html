Living in Columbus

Everyday city services — trash days, parking rules, utilities, voting, and more. Links go directly to official city sources so the info stays current.

🗑️ Trash & Recycling
Find Your Pickup Day
The Columbus Department of Public Works provides weekly residential trash collection. Search your address using the city's interactive map to find your scheduled day.
🔍 Find my pickup day
Recycling Program
The city offers voluntary curbside recycling through Rumpke, which requires a separate signup. Materials are collected bi-weekly on your normal trash day.
♻️ Curbside recycling info
Yard Waste & Brush Pickup
Columbus collects yard waste (grass, leaves, brush) seasonally. Material must be placed in city-approved Toters, compostable bags, or bundled according to size regulations.
🍂 Yard waste rules
Report a City Issue
Use the Columbus Resident Requests portal to report missed garbage collections, potholes, graffiti, or tall grass violations online.
📱 Report an issue
🚗 Parking
Downtown Parking
Downtown Columbus offers a mix of free 3-hour on-street parking and paid daily lots. Ensure you follow posted time limits as they are strictly enforced during business hours.
🅿️ View parking map
Pay a Parking Ticket
You can pay parking citations online through the city's secure portal. Have your ticket or license plate number ready.
💳 Pay a ticket
💧 Water & Utilities
Columbus City Utilities
Columbus City Utilities handles water and sewer services. You can start, stop, or transfer service online, or visit their office on Southwestern Parkway.
💧 Start water service 💳 Pay bill
Electric & Gas Service
Electricity in Columbus is primarily provided by Duke Energy. Natural gas service is managed by CenterPoint Energy. Contact them directly to activate your service.
⚡ Duke Energy 🔥 CenterPoint Gas
🗳️ Voting & Civic Life
Register & Vote
Indiana requires voters to be registered before election day. Use the state's portal to check your status, register, or request an absentee ballot.
✅ Register & check status
Bartholomew County Elections
The Bartholomew County Clerk manages early voting locations, polling sites, and local election results. Check the county site for updated voting center maps.
📍 Local election info
Columbus City Council
The City Council comprises representatives from 5 districts and 2 at-large members. Find your district and contact your representative to get involved in local legislation.
🏛️ City Council details
🏫 Schools & Education
BCSC Public Schools
Bartholomew Consolidated School Corporation (BCSC) is the main public school district. Check their site for enrollment procedures, school boundary maps, and calendars.
📚 Enrollment & info
Early Education & Pre-K
Busy Bees Academy serves as the BCSC Early Education Center. Income-eligible families may also qualify for Head Start programs managed locally.
👶 Pre-K programs
McDowell Education Center
Provides adult education, High School Equivalency (HSE/GED) classes, ESL (English as a Second Language), and career certifications.
🎓 Adult education
🪪 ID, Licenses & Vehicles
Indiana BMV
The Indiana Bureau of Motor Vehicles handles all driver's licenses, state IDs, and vehicle registrations. New residents must obtain an Indiana license within 60 days.
🪪 License & registration
Columbus BMV Branch
The local BMV branch is located at 4445 Ray Boll Blvd. Appointments are highly recommended to avoid long wait times.
📍 Branch hours
📋 Permits & Taxes
Building Permits
Columbus Code Enforcement oversees building permits, inspections, and zoning enforcement. Check before building fences, decks, or additions.
🔨 Permits & zoning
Property Taxes
Property taxes are paid to the Bartholomew County Treasurer. Payments are typically due in two installments (Spring and Fall). View statements and pay online.
💳 Pay property taxes
🐾 Pets & Animals
Columbus Animal Care Services
Handles local pet adoptions, lost and found animals, and investigates animal cruelty or stray complaints within city limits.
🐾 Animal services
Humane Society
The Bartholomew County Humane Society serves the broader county area, offering adoptions and accepting surrendered or stray pets outside city lines.
🐕 Humane Society
🚨 Safety & Emergency
Emergency vs. Non-Emergency
Always dial 911 for life-threatening emergencies. For non-urgent police matters, call the Bartholomew County Emergency Operations Center dispatch at (812) 379-1650.
🆘 911 📞 Non-emergency police
Everbridge Emergency Alerts
Bartholomew County Emergency Management uses Everbridge to send critical weather and safety alerts via text and email. Registration is free.
🔔 Sign up for alerts
🌳 Neighborhood Life
Columbus Parks & Recreation
Manages the People Trail system, community centers, Donner Aquatic Center, and various city parks. Browse and register for community programs online.
⛳ Parks & rec programs
Public Library
The Bartholomew County Public Library offers free library cards to local residents, granting access to books, digital media, study rooms, and community events.
📚 BCPL Website
ColumBUS Transit
The city's public bus system operates 5 fixed routes. Access maps, schedules, and fare information on the city transit page.
🚌 Transit maps

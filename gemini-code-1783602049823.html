Living in Columbus

Everyday city services — trash days, parking rules, utilities, voting, and more. Links go directly to official city sources so the info stays current.

🗑️ Trash & Recycling
Find Your Pickup Day
The City of Columbus Sanitation Department collects residential trash weekly using designated city-issued carts. Residents can view color-coded collection maps and search schedules online.
🔍 Find my pickup day
Recycling Program
Curbside recycling is operated on a bi-weekly basis alongside standard trash services. Residents can recycle clean paper, cardboard, plastics (#1-#7), aluminum, and glass inside the designated recycling container.
♻️ Recycling Info
Bulk & Yard Waste Pickup
The city provides loose leaf collection during autumn months and regular curbside yard waste pickup for grass and limbs. Heavy curbside bulk items require an appointment scheduled via phone.
🍂 Yard waste info
Missed Pickup or Illegal Dumping
If your collection was missed or you spot illegal dumping, submit a report or call the Columbus Sanitation Department directly to coordinate a resolution.
📱 Contact Sanitation
🚗 Parking
Downtown Parking Regulations
Downtown Columbus provides free timed on-street parking alongside multiple public garages. Pay strict attention to posted limits to avoid standard citations from parking enforcement.
🧹 Parking Info
Pay a Parking Ticket
Parking citations are managed through the Columbus Police Department and City Clerk's office. Fines can be processed online or paid in person at City Hall.
💳 Ticket Info
💧 Water & Utilities
Start or Transfer Water Service
Columbus City Utilities handles public water and sewer infrastructure. New accounts require an application form, proof of identification, and a standard deposit processed at their main office.
💧 Start water service
Pay Your Water Bill
Residents can manage monthly utility expenses securely through the online bill-pay portal, by mail, or through direct auto-draft setups.
💳 Pay online
Electric & Gas Service
Electricity is primarily supplied by Duke Energy, while natural gas pipeline connections across Bartholomew County are managed by CenterPoint Energy.
⚡ Duke Energy 🔥 CenterPoint Energy
🗳️ Voting & Civic Life
Register to Vote
Voter registration is handled via the state's online registry portal or locally at the Bartholomew County Clerk's office. Ensure registration is completed at least 29 days before an election.
✅ Register online
Find Your Polling Place
Bartholomew County uses designated voting centers. Eligible residents can verify their active registration status, track sample ballots, and view nearby polling locations using Indiana's central tracker.
📍 Find my polling place
Find Your Council Rep
The Columbus City Council represents distinct geographical districts alongside at-large seats. View the official city maps to discover who represents your address.
🗺️ District map
🏫 Schools & Education
Enroll in Public Schools
Bartholomew Consolidated School Corporation (BCSC) serves public K-12 students in Columbus. Enrollment requires standard residency verifications, health records, and student birth certificates.
📚 Enrollment info
Adult Education & High School Equivalency
The McDowell Education Center provides free adult education courses, career certifications, English language training (ELL), and High School Equivalency (HSE) preparation.
🎓 Adult education
🪪 ID, Licenses & Vehicles
Driver's License & Vehicle Registration
The Indiana Bureau of Motor Vehicles (BMV) operates a branch in Columbus. New residents must update credentials, secure state IDs, and process titles within 60 days of moving.
🪪 Visit Indiana BMV
📋 Permits, Property & Taxes
Building Permits & Zoning
The Columbus-Bartholomew County Planning Department processes zoning variances, commercial developments, and structurally intensive residential improvements.
🔨 Building permits
Property Taxes
Property tax assessments are performed by the county assessor, while payments are processed bi-annually by the Bartholomew County Treasurer's office.
💳 Pay property taxes
🐾 Pets & Animals
Animal Care Services & Licensing
City of Columbus Animal Care Services manages local dog licensing requirements, enforces municipal welfare ordinances, facilitates pet adoptions, and handles stray pick-ups.
🐕 Animal Services
🚨 Safety & Emergency
Emergency Numbers
Always phone 911 for live emergencies. For active local situations requiring non-emergency police dispatch or deputy investigation within county borders, dial the administrative dispatch line.
🆘 911 (Emergencies) 📞 Dispatch: (812) 379-1689
Emergency Alerts
Bartholomew County Emergency Management distributes time-critical severe weather hazards and civil infrastructure warnings through the countywide Everbridge alert network.
🔔 Sign up for alerts
🌳 Neighborhood Life
Parks & Recreation
Columbus Parks and Recreation coordinates community centers, multi-use athletic trails, seasonal swimming pools, public golf layouts, and local activity programs.
⛳ Parks & recreation
Bartholomew County Public Library
Residents can register for a free library card to browse thousands of physical volumes, request digital media checkouts, use computer terminals, or utilize quiet public study rooms.
📚 Get a library card
